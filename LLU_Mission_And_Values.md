## 1. About LLU — Overview

Loma Linda University (LLU) is a **Seventh-day Adventist university** offering a unique learning environment through the integration of health, science, and faith.

Founded in **1905** in Southern California, LLU grew from a vision of health and hope into a world-class academic medical center. Its pioneering efforts in health and wholeness have touched lives worldwide and positioned LLU as a leader in health science research. Today, the university works in service to its communities and underserved populations globally.

**Loma Linda University Health (LLUH)** is a faith-based academic health sciences center encompassing:
- Loma Linda University
- The LLUH hospital system and healthcare services
- The LLU Faculty Medical Group and clinical locations

For more than 120 years, LLUH has combined **teaching, research, and whole-person healthcare** within a Christ-centered mission.

## 4. The Parable of the Good Samaritan — Community Engagement

The Parable of the Good Samaritan *(Luke 10:25–37)* reflects the heart of Adventist community engagement. In Christ's story, a man is injured and ignored by those of his own faith, but a Samaritan stops to care for him — binding his wounds, providing shelter, and ensuring his recovery. Jesus concluded: **"Go and do likewise."**

This parable embodies the Adventist approach: **compassion in action**, serving neighbors with love regardless of their background. Community service is not optional at LLUH — it is central to who they are. Every act of kindness, whether in the hospital, classroom, or community, reflects the shared mission to continue the healing ministry of Jesus Christ.

## 5. Campus Faith & Lifestyle Expectations

Upon enrolling at LLU, students acknowledge the following foundational commitments:

- **Faith** — LLU is committed to faith in God and respects the rich diversity of faiths represented among students. Various spiritual perspectives are viewed as enriching campus life.
- **Chapel** — Midweek University@Worship services are provided for students and faculty and are required for most students.
- **Religion & Ethics Courses** — All program curricula include religion and ethics courses, integrating faith with clinical judgment and whole-person care.
- **Sabbath Observance** — Seventh-day Adventists observe the Sabbath from Friday sundown to Saturday sundown. During these hours, school offices, labs, and libraries are closed.
- **Behavioral Standards** — LLU expects respect for all persons and high standards of personal and professional conduct, including abstinence from alcohol, nicotine, cannabis, and illicit substances.
- **Professional Appearance** — Students are expected to maintain professional standards of dress to prepare for healthcare careers (see Student Handbook for full guidelines).

## 6. Physical Therapy Program — Essential Functions

The DPT program at LLU's School of Allied Health Professions aims to educate competent generalist physical therapists capable of evaluating, managing, and treating patients in current healthcare settings.

The following essential functions are required to complete the program and enter clinical practice. Applicants are not required to disclose disabilities, but are encouraged to discuss concerns with the program director before the interview. Reasonable accommodations may be provided upon request.

> **Note:** Certain chronic or recurrent illnesses, or deficiencies in knowledge, judgment, integrity, or professional attitude that jeopardize patient care may be grounds for course failure or dismissal.

### 6.1 Cognitive Skills
Students must be able to:
- Receive, interpret, remember, and apply information across cognitive, psychomotor, and affective learning domains.
- Perform PT evaluations analyzing physiological, biomechanical, behavioral, and environmental factors in a timely manner.
- Formulate and execute physical therapy management plans based on evaluation data.
- Reassess and revise plans for effective and efficient management.

### 6.2 Psychomotor Skills

**Locomotion:**
- Navigate between lecture, lab, and clinical locations.
- Maneuver in clinical settings to complete assigned tasks.
- Move quickly in emergency situations to protect patients (e.g., from falling).

**Manual Tasks:**
- Maneuver another person's body parts to perform evaluation techniques.
- Operate common tools: goniometers, sphygmomanometers, safety pins, cotton balls, Q-tips.
- Guide, facilitate, inhibit, and resist movement through physical facilitation and inhibition techniques.
- Perform transfers, positioning, exercise, and mobilization techniques (lifting 10–100+ lbs).
- Safely apply and operate evaluation and treatment equipment.
- Maneuver bolsters, pillows, plinths, mats, gait assistive devices, and chairs (10–100 lbs).
- Competently perform and supervise CPR per AHA/ARC guidelines.

**Small Motor/Hand Skills:**
- Legibly record written assignments, evaluations, patient care notes, and chart documentation.
- Detect changes in muscle tone, skin quality, joint play, kinesthesia, and temperature.
- Safely apply and adjust therapeutic modality controls.
- Apply mobilization techniques safely and effectively.

### 6.3 Sensory Skills

**Visual Acuity:**
- Read written materials (textbooks, handouts, patient charts) in English.
- Observe live demonstrations, presentation slides, X-rays, and training videos.
- Assess movement, posture, body mechanics, and gait visually.
- Monitor treatment environment (modality dials, assistive devices, flooring, etc.).
- Detect patient tolerance cues (facial grimaces, muscle twitching, withdrawal, etc.).

**Auditory Acuity:**
- Hear lectures and clinical discussions.
- Distinguish normal from abnormal breathing, lung, and heart sounds using a stethoscope.

### 6.4 Communication Skills
Students must:
- Effectively communicate with students, teachers, patients, staff, and other healthcare personnel — verbally and in writing.
- Receive and interpret written communication in academic and clinical settings in a timely manner.
- Communicate clearly in life-threatening situations within acceptable norms of clinical settings.
- Communicate quickly and efficiently in oral and written English with all members of the healthcare team.

### 6.5 Self-Care
- Maintain general good health to protect the safety of themselves and others.
- Arrange timely transportation and living accommodations to meet academic and clinical obligations.

### 6.6 Effective Learning & Professional Conduct
Students must:
- Demonstrate respect for all individuals regardless of age, race, gender, sexual preference, disease, mental status, lifestyle, or personal values.
- Exhibit appropriate affective behaviors and mental attitudes in compliance with the APTA's ethical standards.
- Sustain the mental and emotional rigors of a demanding academic and clinical program within set time constraints.
- Acknowledge and respect individual values and opinions to foster harmonious working relationships.

> **Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA) Students:** The goal of the PTA program is to prepare PTAs to provide PT interventions under the direction and supervision of licensed physical therapists. The essential functions above apply to PTA students to the degree that the skill falls within the PTA's scope of practice.

## 8. Brief History of the Seventh-day Adventist Church

### 8.1 The Millerite Movement & the Great Disappointment
- **William Miller**, a Baptist preacher, predicted Christ's return around **1843–1844** based on Daniel 8:14.
- Thousands gathered on **October 22, 1844**, expecting the Second Coming.
- Jesus did not return — this became known as the **Great Disappointment** *(cf. Matthew 24:36)*.
- Most left the movement, but a small group returned to Scripture for deeper study.

### 8.2 Ellen G. White
- **Ellen G. White (1827–1915)** experienced visions that helped early Adventist believers understand Scripture more clearly *(Revelation 12:17; 19:10)*.
- Her writings covered Christian living, health, education, and prophecy — consistently pointing to Jesus and Scripture.
- Adventists regard her as a recipient of the **gift of prophecy**.

### 8.3 Church Organization
By **1863**, the church formally organized as the **Seventh-day Adventist Church** with approximately **3,500 members** and **30 ministers**.

**Governance Structure:**

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Local Church | Where members worship and serve |
| Local Conference | A regional group of churches |
| Union Conference | A larger grouping of conferences |
| Division | Major global regions |
| General Conference | Global governing body — headquartered in **Silver Spring, Maryland** |

The General Conference is appointed every five years *(cf. Exodus 18:21–22)* and sets worldwide policy, coordinates mission, and promotes unity.

### 8.4 Global Reach
- The SDA Church is the **most racially and ethnically diverse religious group in the United States**.
- It has grown to more than **23 million members** worldwide.

## 9. Fundamental Beliefs of Seventh-day Adventists

Seventh-day Adventists accept the **Bible as their only creed**. Their fundamental beliefs constitute the church's understanding of Scripture and may be revised at a quinquennial General Conference Session as led by the Holy Spirit.
