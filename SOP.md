**Basis for Interest in the Profession**  
My interest in healthcare was planted early in my childhood, growing up in a family deeply embedded in the healthcare sector. Originally, I envisioned myself becoming a physician. However, as I became heavily involved in competitive sports—eventually serving as the captain of my college's cricket team and playing collegiate basketball—I experienced the world of athletic injuries and sports rehabilitation firsthand. I discovered that physical therapy is uniquely empowering: unlike medical interventions where healing is often passively received by the patient, physiotherapy requires the patient to be an active participant in their own recovery. I fell in love with this collaborative dynamic, realizing that my true calling was helping individuals actively rebuild their own strength, functional independence, and confidence.

**My Purpose**  
My professional purpose is to evolve from a competent practitioner into an autonomous clinical expert who drives evidence-based advancements in physical therapy. Through 1,092 hours of clinical internship in India, I treated patients across diverse demographics—from cardiovascular ICUs to community-based centers like the Amrutvel Old Age Home. These experiences cemented my desire to specialize in advanced clinical practice without losing touch with academic research. My goal is to actively design, test, and implement targeted clinical protocols, analyze intervention efficacy, and publish clinical findings. Long-term, I aim to merge specialized patient care with clinical research across international settings, broadening my perspective before ultimately bringing advanced standards of autonomous practice back to my home country.

**Specific Reasons to Attend Loma Linda University**  
While I have detailed my alignment with LLU’s values and curriculum in other sections of my application, my specific reason for choosing this institution is its unique synthesis of academic rigor, autonomous clinical training, and structured global service. Coming from a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) program in India, I possess a strong foundation in core clinical sciences. However, the U.S. Doctor of Physical Therapy framework emphasizes autonomous diagnostic reasoning, medical screening, and direct-access care. 

Loma Linda University’s Postprofessional DPT program is the perfect bridge for my specific background. What elevates LLU above other institutions for me is that it trains clinicians for this high level of autonomy within an Academic Health Sciences Center that views healing as a true ministry. Furthermore, LLU’s extensive international mission network directly supports my goal of global clinical exposure, allowing me to learn how advanced physical therapy is adapted and delivered across diverse cultural and socioeconomic landscapes. 

**Other Significant Information**  
I believe that effective healthcare requires understanding patients within their broader cultural and holistic contexts. During my undergraduate clinical rotations, I actively sought multidisciplinary collaborations, completing rotations at both an Ayurvedic College and a Homeopathic Medical College. This exposure to integrative medical systems profoundly shaped my open-minded, patient-centered approach to treatment. 

Beyond the clinic, I am a passionate advocate for public health education. As an undergraduate, I directed and performed in street plays (*Nukkad Natak*) to educate the public on first aid, emergency care, and bioethics, winning first place during our college's Bioethics Week. Combined with my leadership experience as General Secretary of the Student Council, these experiences have prepared me to be an active, contributing member of the LLU campus. I look forward to bringing my diverse clinical background, leadership experience, and cultural perspectives to your graduating class.
