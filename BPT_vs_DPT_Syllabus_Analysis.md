# BPT vs. DPT Syllabus Comparison and Course Analysis

This document compares the completed Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) coursework with the prospective selected Post-professional Doctor of Physical Therapy (PTGR) courses. This analysis identifies foundational topics that will be revisited at an advanced level, as well as entirely new subjects introduced in the DPT program, providing cohesive material to guide application essays and statements of purpose.

## 1. Courses Already Studied in BPT (To Be Studied at an Advanced Level in DPT)

The DPT curriculum builds heavily on the clinical and theoretical foundations established during the BPT. The following PTGR courses represent advanced extensions of domains you have already encountered:

**Orthopaedics, Musculoskeletal System & Biomechanics**
- **BPT Foundation:** Orthopaedics & Traumatology (Sem 5), Biomechanics (Sem 3), Physiotherapy in Musculoskeletal Sciences (Sem 7).
- **DPT Progression:**
  - *PTGR 501. Advanced Orthopaedic Specialty Tracks I*
  - *PTGR 505. Orthopaedic Intervention: Regional Interdependency*
  - *PTGR 511. Advanced Clinical Practice I: Orthopaedic Rehabilitation*
  - *PTGR 516 & 517. Movement Science of the Upper & Lower Quarter*
  - *PTGR 573. Pathokinesiology of Gait*
- **Application Context:** Highlight how BPT provided the fundamental diagnostic and treatment frameworks, and express eagerness to dive deeper into regional interdependency, pathomechanics, and targeted advanced interventions.

**Neurology and Rehabilitation**
- **BPT Foundation:** Neurology (Sem 5), Physiotherapy in Neurosciences (Sem 8).
- **DPT Progression:**
  - *PTGR 509. Function-Based Rehabilitation*
  - *PTGR 510. Neurologic Upper Extremity Management*
- **Application Context:** Discuss your BPT exposure to neurological conditions and the desire to master advanced, specialized approaches like NDT (Neuro-Developmental Treatment) and PNF (Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation) for adult neuro-rehabilitation.

**Cardiopulmonary and General Medical Conditions**
- **BPT Foundation:** Physiotherapy in Cardio-Respiratory & General Conditions (Sem 8).
- **DPT Progression:**
  - *PTGR 515. Cardiopulmonary Approaches to Assessment, Wellness, and Disease*

**Manual Therapy**
- **BPT Foundation:** Choice Based Course - Manual Therapy (Sem 7).
- **DPT Progression:**
  - *PTGR 506. Soft-Tissue Mobilization*

**Foundational Sciences & Research**
- **BPT Foundation:** Human Physiology (Sem 1 & 2), Pharmacology (Sem 4), Research Methodology and Biostatistics (Sem 6).
- **DPT Progression:**
  - *PTGR 571. Advanced Physiology I: Neurobiology*
  - *PTGR 577. Pharmacology in Physical Therapy*
  - *PTGR 594. Evidence-Based Practice in Physical Therapy*

## 2. New Specialized Courses in the DPT Program

The DPT introduces several advanced competencies specifically aligned with autonomous practice, leadership, and niche diagnostic skills. These areas are either entirely new or were only superficially covered in the BPT syllabus.

**Autonomous Clinical Reasoning & Medical Screening**
- *PTGR 553. Clinical Reasoning and Critical Thinking in Physical Therapy*
- *PTGR 578. Medical Screening for Physical Therapists*
- *PTGR 513. Advanced Clinical Practice III (Patient classification, algorithms)*
- **Why it matters:** These courses transition a therapist from protocol-driven care to an autonomous practitioner by focusing on advanced decision-making and recognizing non-neuromusculoskeletal origins of pain (red flags requiring direct medical referral).

**Advanced Diagnostics & Imaging**
- *PTGR 579. Clinical Imaging for Physical Therapist*
- **Why it matters:** Moves beyond basic radiographic recognition to utilizing modern imaging (MRI, CT, PET, and clinical ultrasound) directly in physical therapy practice and clinical decision-making.

**Specialized Practice Domains**
- *PTGR 512. Advanced Clinical Practice II (Vestibular disorders)* - Specific focus on dizziness and postural instability.
- *PTGR 529. Integumentary and Lymphatic Systems* - Dedicated interventions for skin protection, wound care, and lymphatic complications.
- *PTGR 519. Home Health Physical Therapy for the Post-Acute Patient* - Focus on Medicare guidelines and specific post-acute setting requirements.

**Healthcare Leadership and Management**
- *PTGR 514. Professional Systems in Management I*
- **Why it matters:** Introduces the business, administrative, and leadership aspects of physical therapy (e.g., personnel selection, finance, team development), which are critical for career progression but rarely emphasized in undergraduate clinical training.

## 3. Application Material Mapping

When drafting application materials, you can structure your narrative as follows:
1. **The Foundation:** Frame your BPT as the solid bedrock that has crafted your baseline clinical competence and exposed you to all major systems (Ortho, Neuro, Cardio).
2. **The Bridging Desire:** Position the DPT program—specifically the progression courses listed in Section 1—as the necessary bridge to achieve clinical mastery in those systems.
3. **The Leap to Independence:** Call out your excitement for the entirely new domains from Section 2, specifically emphasizing a desire to build **autonomous diagnostic capabilities (medical screening, advanced imaging)** and to cultivate **healthcare leadership/management** skills. This shows you understand the purpose of a Post-professional DPT.
